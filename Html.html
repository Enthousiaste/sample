CHUTIYA

CHUTIYA is a Hindi word that has different meanings depending on the context. It can refer to a lock of hair on the top of the head, a lock of hair kept by traditionalist Hindus, or to be under one's thumb, meaning to be under complete control. It can also refer to a derogatory term for a man who lives off his wife's prostitution or someone who kills themselves by "excess of venery," i.e. by catching a sexually transmitted disease. The Chutiya people are an Indigenous Assamese people or an ethnic group originating in the Indian state of Assam and are the descendants of the Sino-Tibetan family of Mongoloid stock. There are three categories of chutia: chutiya, chaman chutiya, and sir chutiya.
